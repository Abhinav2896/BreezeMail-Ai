# Greetings and Sign-Offs Catalog

## Professional Greetings
- "Dear [Name]," — classic, always safe for formal contexts
- "Hello [Name]," — slightly less formal, still professional
- "Good morning/afternoon [Name]," — time-aware, adds a personal touch
- "Dear [Title] [Last Name]," — for executives or first-contact formality

### When Recipient Name is Unknown
- "Hello," — safest generic professional greeting
- "Dear Hiring Manager," — for job applications
- "Dear [Department] Team," — when writing to a group

## Friendly Greetings
- "Hi [Name]!" — warm and approachable
- "Hey [Name]," — familiar but not overly casual
- "Hope you're doing well, [Name]!" — adds warmth
- "Happy [day of week]!" — lighthearted opener for known contacts

### When Recipient Name is Unknown
- "Hi there!" — friendly and inclusive
- "Hello!" — simple and warm
- "Hi everyone," — for group emails

## Casual Greetings
- "Hey [Name]," — the standard casual opener
- "Hey!" — for close colleagues
- "Yo," — only for very close peers in informal cultures
- "Quick one —" — skips the greeting entirely for speed

### When Recipient Name is Unknown
- "Hey there," — casual but not presumptuous
- "Hi all," — for group casual messages

## Apologetic Greetings
- "Dear [Name]," — formality conveys seriousness
- "Hello [Name]," — professional but not cold
- Never open with the apology itself; greet first, then acknowledge

## Assertive Greetings
- "Hello [Name]," — neutral authority
- "[Name]," — direct, implies urgency (use sparingly)
- "Dear [Name]," — formal assertion

---

## Professional Sign-Offs
- "Best regards," — the universal professional default
- "Sincerely," — formal, good for first contacts
- "Kind regards," — slightly warmer than "Best regards"
- "Respectfully," — for sensitive or hierarchical contexts
- "Thank you for your time," — when requesting something

## Friendly Sign-Offs
- "Best," — short, warm, widely accepted
- "Thanks so much!" — adds gratitude
- "Looking forward to hearing from you!" — inviting continuation
- "Warmly," — for nurturing professional relationships
- "Take care," — genuine warmth without over-familiarity

## Casual Sign-Offs
- "Cheers," — the casual standard
- "Talk soon," — implies ongoing relationship
- "Later!" — very informal, close peers only
- "Thanks!" — simple, always works
- "— [Name]" — dash-name, the minimalist approach

## Apologetic Sign-Offs
- "Sincerely," — conveys genuine regret
- "With apologies," — explicit when the mistake was significant
- "Thank you for your patience," — acknowledges the recipient's burden
- "I appreciate your understanding," — for moderate issues
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out," — keeps the door open

## Assertive Sign-Offs
- "Regards," — concise authority
- "Best," — professional without softening
- "Thank you in advance," — implies expectation (use carefully)
- "I look forward to your response by [date]," — sets a deadline politely
