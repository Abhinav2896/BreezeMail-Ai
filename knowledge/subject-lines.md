# Subject Line Best Practices

## Core Principles

### Be Specific
Vague subjects get ignored. Every subject line should answer: "What is this about and why should I care?"

- ❌ "Update" → ✅ "Q3 Marketing Budget: Updated Projections"
- ❌ "Question" → ✅ "Question About the Vendor Contract Timeline"
- ❌ "Hello" → ✅ "Introduction: Sarah Chen ↔ Marketing Team"
- ❌ "Important" → ✅ "Action Required: Server Migration on Friday"

### Keep It Short
- **Target**: 40–55 characters (optimal for mobile and desktop)
- **Maximum**: 60 characters (beyond this, mobile clients truncate)
- **Minimum**: 15 characters (shorter feels incomplete)

### Front-Load Keywords
The most important word should appear in the first 3–4 words. Mobile previews show only ~35 characters.

- ❌ "I Wanted to Check In About the Budget Report"
- ✅ "Budget Report: Quick Check-In"

## Pattern Templates

### Action Requests
- "[Action needed]: [Topic] by [Deadline]"
- "Request: [Specific thing needed]"
- "Please review: [Document/topic]"

### Information Sharing
- "[Topic]: [Key takeaway]"
- "[Project name] Update — [Date or milestone]"
- "Summary: [Meeting/event] on [Date]"

### Follow-Ups
- "Following up: [Original topic]"
- "Re: [Topic] — [New development]"
- "Checking in on [Topic]"

### Introductions
- "Introduction: [Person A] ↔ [Person B]"
- "Connecting you with [Name] re: [Topic]"

### Apologies
- "Apology: [Issue] — [Resolution status]"
- "Correction: [What was wrong] — Updated [thing]"

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **ALL CAPS**: "URGENT: READ THIS NOW" — feels like spam
- **Clickbait**: "You won't believe what happened…" — erodes trust
- **Excessive punctuation**: "Important!!!" — looks unprofessional
- **Empty or single-word subjects**: "Hi" / "Hey" / "FYI" — lazy and unhelpful
- **Re: Re: Re: chains**: Clean up the subject when the topic shifts
- **Emoji in subject lines**: Acceptable in marketing, inappropriate in business email
- **Misleading subjects**: Never use "Re:" to fake a reply thread
